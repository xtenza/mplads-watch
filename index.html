SECTION 06

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about MPLADS and this data.

Each MP gets ₹5 Crore/year to recommend local development works. The MP recommends only. District Authority sanctions and executes. MPs do not handle the money directly.

₹5 Crore per year. Released in two instalments of ₹2.5 Cr directly to the District Authority — not to the MP. Total across ~788 MPs: approximately ₹3,940 Crore per year.

It is non-lapsable — rolls over to next year within the term. No penalty exists for low utilisation. Any balance remaining 18 months after an MP leaves office is returned to the Consolidated Fund of India.

Roads, water, education, health, sanitation, electricity, irrigation, sports, public facilities. Must create durable community assets. Cannot be used for office buildings, land acquisition, grants to individuals, or naming assets after people.

Lok Sabha MPs spend in their own constituency only. Rajya Sabha MPs can spend anywhere in their elected state. Nominated MPs can spend in any state in India. This makes RS and Nominated spending harder to track locally.

45 days: District Authority must sanction or reject.
12 months: Implementing agency must complete the work.
18 months: Works must close after MP's tenure ends.
Tracked on eSAKSHI portal since April 2023.

Two sources:
mplads.gov.in — aggregate data up to 2023.
mplads.mospi.gov.in (eSAKSHI) — work-level data, photos, documents from April 2023 onwards. Real-time updates.

The scheme ran on paper until 2023. No photos, no digital documents existed. eSAKSHI changed this — but only covers works recommended after 1 April 2023. For the 17th Lok Sabha (2019–2024), only 2023–24 has digital evidence.

Our measure of verifiable evidence for completed works. Includes: photo upload rate, document submission rate, and timeline compliance. High = well documented. Low = "completed" is largely self-declared by contractor.

No independent verification exists. "Completed" means the implementing agency marked the work done after final payment. CAG found works marked complete with dates before 1993 — before the scheme even existed. RTI responses show 0% inspection in many districts.

Yes. File with the Nodal District Authority for your MP. Ask for: works sanctioned, amounts spent, completion certificates, inspection reports.

CAG audits periodically. Key findings:

  • Works dated before scheme existed
  • 0% inspection in many districts
  • Utilisation certificates not submitted
  • Action taken on 2001 report submitted 8 years late
